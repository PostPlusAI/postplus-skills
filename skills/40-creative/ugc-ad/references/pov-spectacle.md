# POV Or Spectacle

Template references: `camera_pov`, `ugc_giant_figure`, `classic_meets_modern`.

## Use When

Use when an unusual viewpoint, scale contrast, or elevated scene can create the hook while the product still receives a clear interaction and proof beat.

## Grammar

- Establish the unusual visual rule immediately: fixed POV, scale reveal, or coherent elevated world.
- Keep one visual rule across the clip rather than stacking unrelated spectacle.
- Make the product readable before or during the reveal.
- Follow spectacle with a real handling, use, detail, or result beat.
- End with product behavior or a human verdict, not spectacle alone.

## Camera And Sound

- Fixed POV: hold one camera source and let blocking create motion.
- Scale reveal: begin close and pull back or reframe once.
- Elevated world: use deliberate composition, restrained push-ins, and matching sound or music.

## Proof And Continuity

- Classify spectacle as hook or metaphor unless a literal product action is shown.
- Lock viewpoint, lens behavior, product scale, scene rule, palette, and reveal direction.

## Avoid

- Copying the exact animal, giant object, historical setting, or recognizable scene device.
- Letting production value replace product relevance.
- Presenting a fictional environment as literal performance proof.

## Generator Risks

Extreme lens distortion, fixed-camera blocking, scale consistency, complex environments, crowds, animals, large props, and product legibility during reveals.
