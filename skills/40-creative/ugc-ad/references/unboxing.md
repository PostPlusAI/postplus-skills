# Unboxing

Template references: `ugc_unboxing`, `ugc_unboxing_asmr`, `ugc_unboxing_virtual_try_on`.

## Use When

Use when packaging, arrival, reveal order, material touch, product variety, sound, or immediate try-on contributes to desire.

## Grammar

- Open with package impact, tapping, arrival language, or the package already in hand.
- Reveal one packaging layer per beat.
- Isolate the product after reveal and show one material, texture, hardware, or variety detail.
- End with consumption, use, wear, fit check, or a personal verdict.
- Select ASMR explicitly; do not apply whispering and amplified handling sounds by default.

## Camera And Sound

- Use high-angle hand coverage for package steps and centered close-ups for details.
- Keep the camera mostly static and cut on each physical reveal.
- Record thuds, tearing, sliding, rustling, tapping, biting, or handling sounds that match the action.

## Proof And Continuity

- Preserve package condition from closed to opened states.
- Lock package dimensions, layer order, product count, product color, hands, and reveal surface.

## Avoid

- Showing the final product before the reveal has created anticipation.
- Repeating packaging steps without a new discovery.
- Treating attractive packaging as product-performance proof.

## Generator Risks

Package geometry, nested layers, opening mechanisms, item counts, fingers, material texture, food interiors, and sound synchronization.
