# Social Validation

Template references: `couple_sharing_home`, `ugc_addiction`.

## Use When

Use when desirability is best shown through shared use, handoff, repeated use, another person's reaction, or a believable inability to stop returning to the product.

## Grammar

- Establish one person already using or holding the product.
- Introduce a second use, second person, or repeated reach as the validation event.
- Show direct product handling rather than relying only on dialogue.
- Include one detail or use beat that explains why the product is desirable.
- End with shared attention, repeated behavior, or a recommendation.

## Camera And Sound

- Use medium social framing for the relationship and close inserts for tactile use.
- Follow handoffs with a simple pan or cut.
- Preserve natural dialogue, reactions, and product sounds under light music.

## Proof And Continuity

- Treat the second behavior as social evidence, not independent product-performance proof.
- Lock both identities, seating or blocking, product position, handoff direction, and product state.

## Avoid

- Staged praise without a visible use event.
- Introducing extra people who have no narrative job.
- Exaggerated obsession language for sensitive or regulated products.

## Generator Risks

Multiple-person identity continuity, eye lines, handoff geometry, shared props, dialogue timing, and repeated product state.
