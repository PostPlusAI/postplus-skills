# Try-On And Styling

Template references: `ugc_virtual_try_on`, `ugc_virtual_try_on_sneakers`, `ugc_unboxing_virtual_try_on`.

## Use When

Use for apparel, footwear, accessories, activewear, fit checks, outfit assembly, styling options, and movement-based product proof.

## Grammar

- Open with the item held toward the camera, on a hanger, or just removed from packaging.
- Use a jump cut or direct action to move from item preview to on-body result.
- Show fit through walking, turning, squatting, flexing, fastening, or styling.
- Add one close detail for material, hardware, sole, waistband, or texture.
- End on a composed full or medium-full look and a concise verdict or viewer prompt.

## Camera And Sound

- Keep the main try-on camera static and eye-level.
- Use low close-ups for footwear and medium-full framing for fit and movement.
- Let creator speech or VO explain only the detail currently shown.

## Proof And Continuity

- Lock creator identity, body proportions, base wardrobe, item geometry, colors, room, and light.
- Treat styling as evidence of fit or versatility, not proof of unsupported performance.

## Avoid

- Changing body shape, garment construction, or item color across cuts.
- Showing only poses when the claim depends on movement or fit.
- Overloading one look with unrelated accessories.

## Generator Risks

Outfit continuity, fabric behavior, footwear geometry, feet and hands, body-item contact, jump-cut identity drift, and matched full-body framing.
