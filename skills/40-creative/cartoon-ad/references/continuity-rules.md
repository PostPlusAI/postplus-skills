# Continuity Rules

Use this reference before production handoff and when making the script generation-ready.

## Continuity Locks

Lock only recurring or meaningful elements:

- protagonist face, proportions, material, hair, and core wardrobe
- physical offer geometry, controls, color, and scale
- software UI structure, screen state, and interaction sequence
- service/community participants, setting, and interaction state
- pressure carrier design and behavior
- visual-argument colors, props, particles, or change rules
- recurring location layout, key props, and time of day
- screen direction and entry/exit direction
- pressure-state and result-state lighting
- offer state before, during, and after interaction

Allow nonessential decor, extras, and minor camera-side variation to change.

## Smallest Useful Asset Set

Prepare only assets required by shot dependencies:

- protagonist neutral, pressure, and result references
- clean physical offer or stable UI plate
- offer-interaction keyframe
- recurring location wide plate
- pressure-carrier reference
- visual-argument or mechanism reference
- first-frame candidate
- final offer/CTA plate

For services or communities, add only when required:

- call or meeting composition
- feedback or decision moment
- implementation/result keyframe
- approved customer or testimonial plate

## Model-Facing Prompt Rules

Describe the locked subject, one action, framing, camera movement, environment, lighting state, offer/prop relationship, emotional read, and entering/leaving continuity state.

Keep out:

- copied brand or creator names from references
- named studio or living creator style requests
- unsupported claims
- final legal copy or exact package typography
- dense UI, reviews, prices, and numeric labels
- internal labels such as `proof_plan` or `pressure_carrier`

## Generation Risks

Check for identity, wardrobe, material, hand, offer geometry, UI, recurring-location, particle, scale, mixed-media, unscripted-audio, and claim drift.

For services, also check that the protagonist visibly participates rather than watching a generic meeting screen.

## Mixed-Media Rule

When combining stylized characters with realistic products or UI, bind lens perspective, camera height, scale, contact shadow, color temperature, depth of field, and motion blur. Plan the transition explicitly.
