# Shot Script Rubric

Use this reference when writing or reviewing the cartoon-ad shot table.

## Required Output Shape

Always produce:

`time | visual | framing/composition | camera motion | action | offer/prop relationship | lighting/color | sound | speech/text | purpose`

## Row Standards

Each row must specify one concrete subject, framing relationship, camera movement or `static`, primary action, causal offer/prop role, light/color state, sound source or silence choice, useful text if any, and one narrative purpose.

Write visible production facts, not abstract direction such as "make it cinematic."

## Shot Jobs

Cover only jobs required by the selected engine:

- `hook disruption`: pressure, contradiction, or striking problem visualization
- `pressure definition`: establish the visual argument
- `consequence`: show what pressure changes
- `failed solution`: show prior attempts when relevant
- `mechanism bridge`: create a question the offer can answer
- `offer entry`: motivate appearance
- `offer interaction`: show the protagonist using, joining, asking, configuring, or applying
- `mechanism trace`: visualize operation without overclaiming
- `result behavior`: return to the established protagonist or approved customer
- `proof trace`: show approved evidence and identify its owner
- `identity or CTA close`: complete the state and action

## Timing Guidance

- Hook: 0.8-2.0 seconds per beat.
- Ordinary progression: 1.5-3.0 seconds.
- Interaction or explanation: 2.5-4.5 seconds.
- Proof: 3-6 seconds when readability is required.
- CTA: 3-6 seconds.

Let copy density and readability determine total duration. Do not delay offer interaction so long that no time remains for mechanism, result, and proof.

## Causal Closure Gate

Before approval, mark each item `Pass`, `Missing`, or `Not Applicable`:

- pressure is visible before offer entry
- offer interaction is visible, not merely an appearance
- mechanism trace follows the interaction
- result belongs to the protagonist or an approved customer/testimonial
- result is behavioral or functional, not only a smile, glow, or generic group
- proof status is explicit
- CTA or identity close is complete

Do not call the script complete when a required item is missing. If the source video ends mid-sentence, mid-offer, or before result/CTA, classify it as a likely excerpt before treating the omission as style grammar.

## Quality Checks

- The first three seconds work without a logo-only reveal.
- The visual argument can be understood without subtitles.
- The offer interaction matches `offer_type`.
- Interaction and result are causally connected.
- Aspirational peer imagery is not mislabeled as proof.
- Each location has one narrative job.
- Each shot has one main idea and action.
- Proof does not exceed supplied evidence.
- Generated text does not carry critical package, legal, numeric, price, review, or UI information.
- The concept does not copy a reference's distinctive character or scene.

## Common Repairs

- Generic cartoon: sharpen the pressure carrier and visual argument.
- Lecture: replace explanation with object behavior or character action.
- Random magic: define a stable change rule tied to offer interaction.
- Sudden offer: add a mechanism bridge or motivated entry.
- Passive service screen: show asking, feedback, decision, or implementation.
- Unearned result: return to the protagonist and add interaction plus proof.
- Visual noise: remove secondary effects and reduce camera motion.
- Continuity risk: reduce locations or characters and strengthen locks.

## Approval Prompt

Ask one concise question in the user's language. Do not ask a second confirmation after approval.
