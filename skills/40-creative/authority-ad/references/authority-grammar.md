# Authority Grammar

## Authority Basis

Choose the strongest task-supported basis:

1. `approved_credential`: a verified credential relevant to the claim.
2. `operator_experience`: specific first-hand operational experience approved for use.
3. `demonstration`: the viewer can observe the process, comparison, or result.
4. `source_backed_analysis`: approved data or research supports a clear explanation.
5. `case_narration`: an approved customer or composite case makes the pressure and change concrete.
6. `informed_peer`: a credible user explains a bounded experience without claiming professional authority.

Do not upgrade wardrobe, setting, job title, or confident delivery into a stronger authority basis.

## Delivery Formats

- `direct_explainer`: presenter addresses the viewer and alternates with proof inserts.
- `interviewer_insider`: interviewer surfaces pressure, contradiction, and solution through questions.
- `skeptical_dialogue`: a second speaker states objections and forces clearer proof.
- `podcast_exchange`: matched speaker coverage with evidence B-roll and restrained ambient sound.
- `case_narrative`: narrator follows one burdened person from pressure to mechanism and change.
- `demonstration_expose`: physical comparison, screen use, or mechanism visualization leads the argument.
- `split_screen_deliberation`: stable panels externalize competing opinions or objections.

Choose the format that makes the available authority and proof easiest to understand. Do not choose a format only because it appears in a reference.

## Argument Architecture

Use the smallest sequence that makes the claim causal:

`hook -> recognizable pressure -> accepted assumption -> hidden limitation or mechanism -> evidence -> failed/common alternative -> offer interaction -> result or curiosity close`

Omit beats that are unnecessary or unsupported. A deliberate excerpt may stop at curiosity, but mark it as an excerpt rather than a complete ad.

## Human State

Open on a capable person under observable pressure: wasted time, stalled results, repeated chores, physical difficulty, confusion, skepticism, or work consuming personal life. Move toward clarity, agency, relief, confidence, or a concrete next decision. Avoid helplessness and exaggerated distress unless source evidence requires it.

## Camera And Edit Grammar

- Default to vertical `9:16`.
- Use eye-level medium close-ups or medium shots for speech.
- Use matched profile coverage for dialogue and centered stable panels for split screen.
- Use close-ups for one evidence object: hands, interface, chart, material, packaging, calendar, watch, paperwork, or mechanism.
- Keep cameras locked or nearly locked. Let gestures and hard cuts create pace.
- Use handheld texture only for naturally mobile contexts such as street interviews, commuting, pets, or domestic phone footage.
- Cut when the speaker, claim, example, evidence, or emotional beat changes.
- Use 1-3 second evidence inserts and 2-6 second speech or reaction holds.
- Give numbers, contradictions, and reversals their own readable visual beat.

## Product Behavior

Let early props carry the pressure. Introduce the offer after the problem or mechanism is legible unless the hook is itself a demonstration. Use one simple gesture: tap, open, select, compare, hold, apply, demonstrate, or observe. Prefer real use-state proof over ornamental pack shots.

## Sound And Text

Keep speech foregrounded. Use low continuous music when it supports the format; clean dialogue and room tone are valid. Use phrase-level subtitles tied to speech. Reserve large emphasis for the hook, a number, or the central reversal. Render exact text in post.
