# Proof And Claims

## Proof Stack

Prefer at least two compatible proof layers when the brief supports them:

- `observable_symptom`: a visible burden, failure, or consequence.
- `physical_demonstration`: an action or comparison the viewer can inspect.
- `interface_or_process_state`: an approved screen, workflow, or operational result.
- `approved_number`: a sourced time, cost, performance, or outcome statement.
- `approved_testimonial`: a real, permissioned statement kept within its source wording.
- `approved_research`: a relevant study or dataset represented accurately.
- `mechanism_illustration`: clearly illustrative visualization of how something may work.
- `social_agreement`: another speaker confirms a perception; use as reaction, not scientific proof.

Build the stack as `symptom -> mechanism -> evidence/use-state` when possible.

## Status Labels

Classify every material claim internally:

- `approved`: supported and cleared for the intended wording.
- `needs_source`: plausible but no task-local substantiation is available.
- `illustrative_only`: useful for explanation but not literal proof.
- `testimonial_only`: bounded to one approved person's experience.
- `prohibited`: conflicts with known facts, policy, or claim boundaries.

Do not soften `needs_source` into factual copy. Rewrite to a bounded observation or stop and request the missing fact when it changes the ad.

## Authority Integrity

- Verify professional titles, credentials, employers, customer status, and experience claims before using them.
- Do not imply that an actor is a licensed professional or that a studio conversation is an independent endorsement.
- Treat lab coats, scrubs, bookshelves, microphones, clinics, graphs, and technical animation as visual cues only.
- Distinguish an approved real case from a composite or fictional scenario.
- Do not create fake reviews, fake dashboards, fake study graphics, fake before/after states, or invented customer histories.

## Sensitive Claims

Require explicit approved wording and substantiation for medical, physiological, legal, financial, safety, comparative, performance, ingredient, price, review-count, guarantee, and quantified outcome claims.

Keep mechanism visuals within the approved claim. Use neutral labels such as `illustrative visualization` in the production plan where needed. Do not ask a generator to create precise scientific, financial, legal, or UI evidence that does not already exist as an approved asset.

## Claim Placement

- Put the strongest approved claim beside its proof beat.
- Keep disclaimers, citations, exact numbers, charts, and legal text in post-production layers.
- Do not bury a claim correction later in the ad.
- Do not let a dramatic visual imply a stronger outcome than the spoken wording.
