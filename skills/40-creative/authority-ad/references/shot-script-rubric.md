# Shot Script Rubric

## Row Contract

Write one dominant action and one argumentative job per row.

- `time`: fit the spoken clause and visible action; use 1-3 seconds for inserts and 2-6 seconds for speech or reaction.
- `visual`: state only what appears on screen.
- `framing/composition`: name shot size, angle, subject placement, and subtitle-safe space when relevant.
- `camera motion`: use static, slight handheld, short pan, push, or tracking only when motivated.
- `action`: describe one physically executable subject or prop action.
- `offer/prop relationship`: state whether the prop carries pressure, mechanism, proof, offer use, or result.
- `lighting/color`: use plausible source-motivated lighting and readable color.
- `sound`: identify speech posture, room tone, music intent, and essential diegetic sound.
- `speech/text`: write approved on-camera or VO copy; keep subtitle behavior concise and move exact graphics to post.
- `purpose`: name the row's argument job, not a vague mood.

## Script Checks

- The first 1-3 seconds create a question, contradiction, pressure, or visible consequence.
- Every insert directly supports the current spoken claim.
- The authority basis is clear without depending on costume or set dressing.
- Skepticism, when used, forces a meaningful clarification rather than filler banter.
- The offer enters through a plausible interaction after enough context exists.
- The mechanism and proof do not exceed approved claims.
- The result returns to human behavior, state, or decision.
- The cut pattern is varied by argument, not by decorative transition.
- Exact captions, logos, UI, prices, reviews, charts, and legal copy are reserved for post.

## Causal Closure Gate

Mark each item `Pass / Partial / Missing`:

- pressure is visible
- accepted assumption is identifiable
- hidden limitation or mechanism is explained
- proof supports the mechanism or offer capability
- offer interaction is visible and plausible
- result follows from the shown interaction
- final human state or decision is embodied
- CTA or curiosity close matches the ad's completion status
