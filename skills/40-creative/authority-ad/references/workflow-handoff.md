# Production Handoff

## Shared Locked Context

Pass the approved `Authority Grammar Plan`, shot table, authority basis, proof status, claim boundaries, approved copy, non-imitation constraints, product facts, and provided references. Preserve the distinction between evidence assets and illustrative assets.

## image-batch-runner

Use first when continuity assets are missing.

Request:

- speaker/persona lock without copying a reference creator
- first-frame candidates combining a face with a pressure carrier or contradiction
- pressure-state lifestyle frames
- claim-specific evidence or prop inserts
- product-in-use frames
- clean device plates for later UI compositing
- approved mechanism diagrams or illustrative states when needed

Lock identity, wardrobe by location block, product appearance, prop design, aspect ratio, subtitle-safe space, lighting direction, device orientation, and authority basis. Allow background detail, gesture, expression intensity, and non-material props to vary.

## video-batch-runner

Use when approved assets and prompts are stable.

For each clip pass one narrative job, one visible action, framing, camera behavior, subject state, lighting, speech posture, sound intent, continuity bindings, and asset bindings. Default to locked camera with natural subject motion. Preserve dialogue eyelines, microphone placement, hand-object contact, wardrobe, prop state, UI state, and emotional progression.

Keep exact subtitles, logos, charts, packaging text, legal copy, and detailed UI out of generated footage unless the provider route and approved assets can preserve them reliably.

## Routing Rule

- Missing continuity stills: `$image-batch-runner` then `$video-batch-runner`.
- Stable visual and audio assets: `$video-batch-runner` directly.

Do not create an extra production brief or ask for a second generation confirmation after approval.
