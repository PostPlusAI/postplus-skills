# Shot Script Rubric

## Required Table

Use exactly:

`time | visual | framing/composition | camera motion | action | sound | subtitle/text | proof duty | purpose`

## Row Rules

- Give every row a concrete time range.
- Describe what the viewer sees before explaining why it matters.
- Specify framing distance, body/concern placement, and camera source.
- Distinguish physical camera movement from subject movement, digital crop, or
  edit transition.
- Use one primary action per shot.
- State whether the product is absent, revealed, operating, or recalled.
- Assign a proof duty: `problem`, `inspection`, `operation`, `routine`, `result`,
  `comparison`, or `recall`.
- Put exact approved speech or text in the relevant row. Mark unapproved copy as
  a draft.
- Keep arrows, circles, split screens, labels, captions, disclaimers, and end
  cards in post-production.
- Make every shot plausible for one phone mount/operator and the available hands
  and props.

## Required Sequence Logic

Include at least:

1. Inspectable problem evidence.
2. A human recognition or inspection gesture.
3. A product operation gesture.
4. A distinct result view.

Add routine context, comparison, or product recall only when it improves proof
or comprehension.

## Comparison Control

For matched proof shots, specify:

- camera side and height
- subject distance and crop
- concern location
- posture and expression
- lighting direction and intensity
- wardrobe variables
- allowed and forbidden changes

Do not present posture, compression clothing, makeup, exposure, smoothing, or
camera-angle changes as product results.

## Production Locks

After the shot table, define:

- `persona_lock`: identity, age presentation, face, hair, body proportions,
  skin tone, and stable accessories
- `concern_map_lock`: location, appearance, severity, and which shots show it
- `product_lock`: approved reference, geometry, colors, label policy, texture,
  and operating state
- `scene_lock`: primary room, secondary context, light, camera side, and decor
  boundaries
- `before_after_match_lock`: fixed comparison variables and allowed changes
- `audio_lock`: music/VO/dialogue mode, voice duty, and pacing
- `claim_lock`: approved facts, testimonial wording, and prohibited claims
- `post_only_elements`: captions, arrows, circles, split screens, end cards,
  disclaimers, logos, and other editor-owned graphics

## Review Checklist

Before requesting approval, confirm:

- the problem reads without relying on narration
- the product enters for a believable reason
- the product action is physically possible
- the result is clearly different but not generator-exaggerated
- the comparison remains honest
- the emotional transition follows the evidence
- the text system is singular and restrained
- no creator-specific or unlicensed reference detail is copied
- no unsupported claim, timeline, dosage, or medical implication was added
