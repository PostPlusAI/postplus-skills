# Before-After Style Grammar

Use these rules as a production grammar, not a fixed template. Preserve the
proof logic while varying persona, product, concern, setting, sound, and text
mode.

## Human Arc

Build the visible state change as:

`recognition → practical routine → relief`

Keep the starting emotion specific and restrained: self-consciousness,
frustration, surprise, or inconvenience. End with composure or quiet confidence,
not theatrical celebration.

## Camera And Framing

- Use vertical phone-native framing.
- Keep the concern inside the center third and large enough to inspect.
- Use static or lightly handheld capture.
- Let subject actions create movement: point, touch, turn, lift, apply, scoop,
  consume, wear, rest, or present.
- Use close framing for baseline, application, texture, and result inspection.
- Widen once only when it proves routine integration or environmental ease.
- Return to a clear medium or close view for the result.
- Allow a deliberate digital punch-in or annotation when a subtle concern would
  be missed on a phone screen.

## Proof Routes

### Direct Comparison

Use when the concern can be shown from a repeatable angle.

`baseline detail → product operation → matched result → optional split view`

Lock camera side, distance, concern location, posture, and evidence lighting.
Wardrobe may vary only when it does not create the apparent result.

### Discovery

Use when a photo, mirror, recording, or ordinary moment reveals the concern.

`external trigger → private inspection → product operation → result`

Use generic or owned source material. Do not reproduce recognizable event,
broadcast, platform, or creator details from a reference.

### Routine Montage

Use when product ease or hands-free use is part of the value.

`concern close-up → operating product → medium routine view → wider context → result portrait`

Keep the product active and physically consistent across routine shots.

## Edit Rhythm

- Hook within 1–2 seconds. Allow up to 5 seconds only for a useful discovery
  setup.
- Use 1–3 second beats for problem, use, and comparison.
- Hold result or closing gesture for 3–5 seconds when needed.
- Prefer hard cuts.
- Increase density around solution and comparison, then settle for the result.
- Bridge the montage with one continuous audio logic.

## Lighting And Color

- Prefer available daylight or bright home light that keeps evidence readable.
- Use neutral domestic color with controlled wardrobe or product contrast.
- Allow darker treatment shots only when operating light is itself evidence.
- Preserve natural texture.
- Avoid beauty diffusion, heavy grading, crushed shadows, and lighting changes
  that exaggerate the result.

## Sound Modes

Choose one:

- `music-led`: rhythmic bed plus sparse text
- `testimonial`: first-person VO/on-camera speech plus restrained music
- `dialogue-led`: original or cleared conversational/comedic audio across cuts

Do not copy reference music, dialogue, or recognizable voices.

## Text Modes

Choose one:

- `sparse phrases`: short problem, reassurance, use, and result phrases
- `kinetic captions`: one or a few spoken words at a time
- `opening label only`: one problem phrase, then visual storytelling

Keep text away from the concern, hands, product, and result. Add it in post.

## Product Behavior

- Introduce the product after the concern is legible.
- Show one operational gesture and one recall gesture.
- Prefer application, consumption, dispensing, wearing, or texture handling over
  a product-only packshot.
- Let the product disappear from the result frame when the result itself carries
  proof.

## Proof Integrity

- Treat before/after as personal visual testimony unless stronger substantiation
  is provided.
- Do not infer causality from temporal order.
- Do not invent dosage, duration, diagnosis, treatment, cure, fat loss, or
  guaranteed efficacy.
- Identify variables that can change appearance: angle, distance, posture,
  wardrobe compression, lighting, makeup, hydration, retouching, and time.
- Control or disclose material variables.
- Never create a visible in-shot transformation or morph.

## Generator Risks

Lock against:

- identity drift
- changing concern location or severity
- unstable product geometry, label, texture, cap, or dosage form
- body/skin morphing, flicker, and over-smoothing
- inconsistent hands, anatomy, wardrobe, room, and lighting
- impossible device glow or product interaction
- generated captions, logos, comparison graphics, or legal copy
- unsupported claims leaking into model-facing prompts
