# Street Interview Grammar

Use this reference to design the `Street Interview Grammar Plan`.

## Human Arc

Build from a person who is socially open but not pre-sold:

`interruption or question -> listening -> candid answer or skepticism -> explanation or proof -> qualified interest, conviction, or unresolved curiosity`

Preserve micro-states: pause, glance toward the interviewer, half-smile, hesitation, shrug, laugh, or qualification. Do not replace them with constant enthusiasm.

## Camera And Framing

- Default to vertical medium or medium-close framing.
- Keep the respondent centered or slightly offset toward an off-camera interviewer.
- Use a visible small microphone or interviewer hand at a lower side edge.
- Use two-shots when the exchange matters and singles when the answer or reaction matters.
- Keep plausible public-place depth: pedestrians, parked vehicles, buildings, foliage, or street furniture.
- Use close punch-ins only for surprise, skepticism, a short answer, or a key objection.

The camera should feel nearby and participatory: handheld with low-amplitude drift and imperfect stabilization. Avoid elaborate crane, drone, orbit, or glossy gimbal choreography.

## Movement And Edit

Cut on conversational function: question, reaction, pressure, objection, explanation, proof, response.

- Hooks and reactions: about 1-3 seconds.
- Substantive answers: about 3-7 seconds.
- Extended testimony: about 4-11 seconds with pauses removed.
- Product or proof inserts: about 1-3 seconds per action.
- Preferred mechanics: hard cut, jump cut, short pan/reframe, digital punch-in/out, direct B-roll insert.

Carry dialogue over proof inserts when it preserves clarity. Avoid decorative transitions and cutting every word into a new shot.

## Question Ladder

Use only the steps the concept needs:

1. `hook question`: specific, answerable, and relevant to the audience.
2. `lived pressure`: invite a concrete experience, failed workaround, or opinion.
3. `skeptical follow-up`: voice the viewer's strongest reasonable objection.
4. `solution prompt`: create a natural reason to introduce the offer.
5. `proof prompt`: ask what changed, how it works, or what the person actually did.
6. `qualification`: allow limits, nuance, or uncertainty.
7. `close`: product name, next step, or unresolved cliffhanger when appropriate.

Do not make the interviewer a passive prompt dispenser. Each question should change the argument.

## Product And Prop Behavior

Treat the microphone as the constant conversational prop. Delay the promoted product until pressure or curiosity exists.

Use one plausible product gesture:

- `physical_product`: hold, pass, open, smell, taste, pour, stir, wear, or compare
- `software`: show a supplied UI plate, tap, scroll, select, or review an output
- `service`: ask, receive advice, review a decision, or implement a step
- `community`: ask a question, receive responses, or apply a shared practice
- `content`: read, watch, save, learn, or demonstrate an applied method

A product appearing is not proof. Show use, response, or an approved result trace.

## Lighting, Color, And Sound

Use plausible daylight: soft and cool under overcast skies or bright and contrasty in direct sun. Prioritize believable skin tones over a stylized grade. Controlled inserts may be cleaner and brighter.

Keep live dialogue intelligible over light traffic, pedestrians, wind, or general city ambience. Music is optional, upbeat when used, and subordinate. Preserve useful breaths, laughter, and conversational timing.

## Text Behavior

Use near-verbatim phrase captions in a mobile-safe region. Add at most one persistent premise header or one late CTA layer. Keep final captions, domains, package copy, UI labels, offers, and legal text out of model-generated frames.

## Valid Variants

- one respondent or a reaction montage
- interviewer visible, partially visible, or off camera
- continuous take, jump-cut interview, or interview/proof hybrid
- physical product, software capture, contextual B-roll, or verbal-only offer
- short cliffhanger, standard 30-second ad, or extended interview
- 9:16 or placement-required 4:5

Treat studio segments, aspirational background objects, backward tracking, multi-person scent/reaction montages, and split-screen UI as optional modules, not core requirements.
