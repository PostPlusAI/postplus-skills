# Continuity And Proof

Use this reference to define respondent truth status, proof status, continuity locks, claim boundaries, and generation risks.

## Respondent Truth Status

Declare one status before writing first-person speech:

- `approved_customer`: supplied, consented customer account; use only approved wording
- `approved_expert`: supplied, qualified expert; preserve approved scope and disclosure
- `spokesperson`: brand or creator voice; do not imply independence
- `actor`: performs a disclosed or clearly dramatized scenario; do not imply real results
- `illustrative_persona`: fictional representative used to explain a situation; do not present as testimony

When status is missing, write a spokesperson or illustrative format without personal outcome claims, or ask if the distinction changes the concept.

## Proof Classes

Classify each proof beat:

- `behavioral_proof`: established person performs a previously difficult action
- `functional_proof`: offer visibly performs an approved function
- `sensory_proof`: visible or audible texture, preparation, handling, or immediate reaction
- `process_proof`: approved workflow, ingredient, manufacture, coaching, or method
- `social_proof`: approved customer, testimonial, review, or sourced public response
- `numeric_proof`: supplied result, quantity, duration, price, comparison, or guarantee
- `illustrative_preview`: desirable possibility that proves nothing

For every proof, state `result_owner`, `proof_status: approved | illustrative | missing`, and `source_basis`.

Do not treat an actor's reaction, generic crowd agreement, aspirational peer, generated before/after, or unsourced statistic as proof.

## Continuity Locks

Lock only what must persist:

- respondent identity, truth status, wardrobe, accessories, and handedness
- interviewer screen side, voice, hand, and microphone design
- eyeline targets, height relationship, and screen direction
- street plate, time of day, weather, and light direction
- product geometry, color, orientation, fill level, and handling owner
- prior and next action for every clip
- caption-safe region and final aspect ratio
- supplied UI, audio, voice, and approved claim bindings

## Claim Boundaries

Treat health, physiological, diagnostic, financial, earnings, rapid-result, educational outcome, comparison, ingredient, performance, environmental, price, review, scarcity, discount, endorsement, and guarantee language as claims.

- Use only supplied and approved wording.
- Keep qualifications attached to the claim.
- Do not imply medical, regulatory, professional, or independent-public endorsement.
- Do not show a result that exceeds the approved claim.
- Add final claims, disclaimers, numbers, package text, reviews, UI, and subtitles in post.

## Generation Risk Check

Check:

- two-person identity, height, eyeline, and screen-direction drift
- duplicated microphones, warped fingers, changing hand ownership, and impossible mic placement
- lip-sync or overlapping-dialogue failure
- product/package geometry and fill-level drift
- street background, vehicle, pedestrian, sign, and architecture morphing
- direct-sun exposure and skin-tone shifts
- supplied UI becoming unreadable or falsely branded
- studio-clean audio that does not match the public scene
- generated reactions implying real testimony or endorsement

Repair complex beats by splitting them into stable single-action clips and binding approved still, product, scene, UI, and audio references.
