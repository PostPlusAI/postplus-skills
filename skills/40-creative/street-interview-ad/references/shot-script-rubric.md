# Shot Script Rubric

Use this reference when writing or reviewing the street-interview shot table.

## Required Output Shape

Always produce:

`time | visual | framing/composition | camera motion | subject/action | product/prop relationship | lighting/color | edit | sound/music | speech/text | purpose`

## Row Standards

Each row must specify:

- one concrete visible beat
- respondent and interviewer placement or off-camera relationship
- camera movement or `static`
- one primary human or product action
- microphone, product, phone, UI, or proof-prop role
- plausible light and color state
- edit mechanic and approximate duration
- live sound source, ambience, music, or intentional silence
- exact spoken line needed for timing and only useful post-production text notes
- one conversational or production purpose

Write observable production language. Avoid vague labels such as `authentic`, `premium`, or `cinematic` without visible support.

## Shot Jobs

Cover only the jobs needed by the selected question ladder:

- `hook interruption`
- `listening state`
- `candid answer or pressure`
- `skeptical follow-up`
- `solution entry`
- `product gesture`
- `proof insert or proof answer`
- `qualification or reaction`
- `changed state`
- `optional CTA or cliffhanger`

The product must not enter before the conversation gives it a reason unless the sensory reaction itself is the hook.

## Timing Check

- Keep spoken density plausible for the target duration.
- Give questions and reactions enough time to read as separate human turns.
- Do not script overlapping dialogue unless the production route can support it.
- Use shorter proof inserts under continuous interview audio rather than repeating the explanation.
- End each generated clip on an action or expression that can cut cleanly into the next beat.

## Pre-Approval Gate

Confirm:

- The opening creates a specific viewer question within 1-3 seconds.
- The respondent visibly listens before answering.
- The question ladder advances rather than repeating the same claim.
- Eyeline, screen direction, microphone owner, and hand position are plausible.
- The offer enters through a natural conversational or physical cue.
- Every claimed result has supplied approval or is marked illustrative/missing.
- The result belongs to the established respondent or an approved proof source.
- Captions, hook text, package copy, domains, and UI are reserved for post.
- The final beat resolves the human question or intentionally creates a cliffhanger.

## Common Repairs

- If it feels scripted, add a listening beat, qualification, or concrete follow-up instead of filler slang.
- If it feels like a feature list, turn the strongest feature into a viewer objection and one proof action.
- If the product feels sudden, add the question that gives the product a reason to enter.
- If proof feels weak, replace broad praise with visible use, immediate reaction, or approved task/result evidence.
- If generation is fragile, separate two-person dialogue and product handling into simpler clips with stable reference assets.
