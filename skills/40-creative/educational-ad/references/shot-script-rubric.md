# Shot Script Rubric

Use this reference when writing or reviewing the educational-ad shot table.

## Required Output Shape

Always produce:

`time | visual | framing/composition | camera motion | action | offer/prop relationship | lighting/color | sound | speech/text | evidence job | purpose`

## Row Standards

Each row must specify:

- one concrete visible subject
- a framing relationship and camera movement or `static`
- one primary action or micro-action
- a causal offer or prop role
- a functional light and color state
- a sound source or silence choice
- exact approved speech or a clear copy intention
- one evidence job: `question`, `pressure`, `reason`, `consequence`, `comparison`, `demonstration`, `substantiation`, `result`, or `decision`
- one narrative purpose

Write visible production facts. Avoid vague direction such as `premium`, `cinematic`, `authentic`, or `scientific` without concrete support.

## Shot Jobs

Cover only jobs required by the selected engine:

- `hook question`: establish pressure, contradiction, or comparison
- `pressure definition`: make the problem observable
- `authority or narrative anchor`: establish who is explaining and why
- `reason or mechanism`: name the supported cause or distinction
- `consequence`: show what the reason changes
- `objection or failed assumption`: make skepticism explicit when useful
- `offer entry`: motivate appearance through the question
- `offer interaction`: show handling, use, configuration, or inspection
- `proof trace`: show approved evidence and its owner
- `result behavior`: return to the user, customer, operator, or system
- `decision or CTA close`: complete the knowledge and action

## Timing Guidance

- Hook beat: `0.8-2.0s`.
- Evidence or consequence insert: `1-3s`.
- Anchor statement: `3-6s`.
- Demonstration or comparison: `2.5-5s`.
- Readable proof or CTA: `3-6s`.

Let copy density and evidence readability determine duration. Do not compress proof into unreadable flashes or preserve a long lecture when a direct insert would clarify it.

## Causal Closure Gate

Mark each item `Pass`, `Missing`, or `Not Applicable`:

- a viewer question exists before offer emphasis
- the reason or comparison is understandable
- each material claim has an evidence owner
- evidence shots match the exact spoken clause
- offer entry answers the established question
- offer interaction is visible
- result follows interaction and belongs to the established user or system
- proof status is explicit
- decision, identity close, or CTA completes the argument

Do not call the script complete when a required item is missing.

## Quality Checks

- The first three seconds work without a logo-only or package-only reveal.
- The ad teaches one central idea rather than listing unrelated features.
- The anchor does not imply unsupported credentials.
- B-roll is semantically specific, not generic topic footage.
- Camera motion does not compete with speech, action, text, or proof.
- Product handling is physically plausible and visually inspectable.
- Generated text does not carry critical legal, numeric, package, price, review, guarantee, or UI information.
- The resolved state is behavioral or functional, not only a smile.
- Reference identities, scenes, and distinctive devices are not copied.

## Common Repairs

- Dry lecture: add clause-matched evidence and a visible pressure carrier.
- Random montage: assign every insert an evidence job and remove unmatched footage.
- Product-first pitch: establish the question and reason before the reveal.
- False authority: replace costume signals with a truthful role and owned evidence.
- Weak proof: show interaction, inspection, comparison, or a documented result.
- Claim overload: keep one teaching idea and move unsupported statements out.
- Visual noise: reduce overlays, effects, and camera motion.
- Continuity risk: reduce recurring people or locations and strengthen locks.

## Approval Prompt

Ask one concise question in the user's language. Do not ask a second confirmation after approval.
