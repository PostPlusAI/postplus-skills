# Review Rubric

Use this reference after `$video-analysis` supplies observable shot and audio evidence.

## Evidence First

Report visible and audible facts before interpretation. Preserve uncertainty. Do not infer credentials, claims, results, or offer behavior that the source does not show.

## Classification

Identify:

- primary education engine
- anchor type
- evidence system
- offer first-appearance time
- offer first-interaction time
- likely `complete_ad`, `excerpt`, or `incomplete`

## Audit Scale

Rate each item `Strong`, `Partial`, `Weak`, or `Missing` and cite timecodes:

- specific viewer question or pressure
- stable and truthful anchor
- clear reason, mechanism, or comparison
- clause-matched visual evidence
- edit rhythm and visual variety
- motivated offer entry
- visible offer interaction
- proof ownership and claim alignment
- changed human or system state
- completed decision, close, or CTA
- readable text and intelligible sound
- continuity and non-imitation safety

## Claim Audit

For each material claim, identify:

- spoken or visible wording
- supporting shot or source evidence
- status: `supported in ad`, `asserted only`, `uncertain`, or `outside supplied evidence`

Do not treat a lab coat, podium, audience, animation, product label, or emotional reaction as independent substantiation.

## Causal Closure

Check whether the ad visibly connects:

`pressure -> reason -> consequence -> offer interaction -> proof -> result -> decision`

Mark omitted steps as valid only when the selected engine does not need them. If the source ends mid-argument, classify it as an excerpt before treating omissions as deliberate grammar.

## Amendments

Recommend the smallest precise change:

- replace or add a specific evidence shot
- move offer entry later or earlier
- shorten an anchor hold
- remove an unsupported claim
- add an interaction or result owner
- simplify text or overlays
- repair continuity or sound

Do not rewrite or produce the ad unless requested.
