# Proof Test

Template reference: `crush_test`.

## Use When

Use when a supplied product fact can be demonstrated through visible stress, resistance, recovery, sealing, protection, or uninterrupted physical evidence.

## Grammar

- Open with the product already inside or beside the test condition.
- Hold the main test long enough to preserve credibility.
- Show recovery, retrieval, opening, or inspection after the stress event.
- End with usable product contents or an unambiguous post-test state.

## Camera, Edit, And Sound

- Use stable centered studio framing and a clean product silhouette.
- Cut only when the physical phase changes.
- Emphasize the real action sounds: impact, pressure, crack, hiss, tear, pour, or handling.

## Proof And Continuity

- Require an approved literal property before presenting the test as proof.
- Lock package geometry, initial condition, test apparatus, product state, light, and camera angle.

## Avoid

- Hidden cuts that undermine the test.
- Stress conditions unsupported by product facts.
- Confusing package survival with product-performance claims.

## Generator Risks

Fire, ice, pressure, deformation, melting, liquids, package continuity, hand safety, and realistic physics.
