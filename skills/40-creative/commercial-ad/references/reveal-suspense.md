# Reveal And Suspense

Template reference: `mystery_box`.

## Use When

Use when concealment, a simple choice, an unopened container, delayed information, or autonomous product behavior can create a clear viewer question.

## Grammar

- State or show the choice or concealed object immediately.
- Preserve the container and interaction in one coherent space.
- Delay the reveal only while anticipation is increasing.
- Make the product's first visible action the payoff when possible.
- Capture a human reaction only after the reveal is legible.

## Camera, Edit, And Sound

- Prefer a continuous or lightly cut take with restrained handheld movement.
- Keep hands, container opening, and product emergence visible.
- Let dialogue establish the premise; use real opening and product sounds for payoff.

## Proof And Continuity

- Treat autonomous behavior as functional proof only when supplied product facts support it.
- Lock container dimensions, hand positions, product orientation, reveal direction, and subject blocking.

## Avoid

- Excess setup before the viewer understands the question.
- Cutting away during the reveal.
- A reaction that arrives before the product is readable.

## Generator Risks

Container geometry, hands, concealed-to-visible continuity, autonomous motion, object collisions, and believable reactions.
