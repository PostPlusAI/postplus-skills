# Editorial Try-On

Template reference: `virtual_try_on`.

## Use When

Use for fashion, footwear, bags, accessories, and apparel concepts where movement, material detail, environment, and identity are the main argument.

## Grammar

- Introduce the body and complete look in motion.
- Alternate full or medium-full walking views with close material, hardware, fastening, bag, or footwear details.
- Use location progression only when it strengthens the same identity and palette.
- End on a composed still or slow movement that presents the complete look.

## Camera, Edit, And Sound

- Use low-angle tracking, side tracking, detail inserts, and occasional wide environmental composition.
- Cut rhythmically with walking and reserve longer holds for the final identity frame.
- Layer footsteps, zips, fabric, or hardware sounds over a restrained beat.

## Proof And Continuity

- Treat wear, fit, movement, and material behavior as lifestyle evidence.
- Lock persona, body proportions, full outfit, accessory placement, product geometry, palette, and time of day.

## Avoid

- Random location changes, outfit mutation, or detail shots unrelated to the hero product.
- Calling fashion imagery proof of unsupported comfort or durability.
- Overusing slow motion or lens flare.

## Generator Risks

Full-body identity, clothing and bag continuity, walking physics, feet, hands, hardware detail, city tracking, and changing sunlight.
