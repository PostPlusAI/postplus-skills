# Professional Memo Style

This reference captures a Xiaohongshu note style the user explicitly wants to preserve as a reusable benchmark.

## Target Feel

- professional
- experience-dense
- trust-building
- human
- judgment-led

It should feel like:

"someone who has actually done the work is finally telling you where the real problem is."

It should not feel like:

- corporate content marketing
- textbook teaching
- polished but generic AI writing
- soft lifestyle copy

## What Makes It Work

### 1. Judgment comes first

The writing does not open with background context.

It opens with:

- a hard-earned conclusion
- a recurring problem
- a strong emotional reaction to seeing the same mistake again

Examples of opening energy:

- "为什么越优化结果反而越来越难看"
- "我已经累了，真的累了。"
- "做了这么久，我才知道自己一直在看假数据"

### 2. Lived experience is visible

The authority comes from repeated exposure, not from title-dropping.

Useful language patterns:

- "我见过太多次"
- "每次打开一个新账户"
- "说实话"
- "第一反应是..."

### 3. Consequences are concrete

Instead of abstract advice, the note shows:

- what goes wrong
- what it costs
- why the platform or system gets misled

This keeps the note grounded in operational reality.

### 4. The tone is blunt but not noisy

It uses emotion sparingly and purposefully:

- frustration
- fatigue
- disbelief
- warning

That gives the post force without turning it into rant content.

### 5. Structure serves scanning

The body is easy to screenshot and skim:

- short paragraphs
- checklist-like progression
- one idea block at a time

This is important for Xiaohongshu memo posts.

## Writing Rules

When drafting in this style:

- prefer short sentences over long explanations
- prefer consequence over theory
- prefer real operator language over platform jargon for its own sake
- use jargon only when it changes meaning
- let one sharp line carry a paragraph when possible
- avoid overused contrast templates like `不是……而是……`
- avoid bridge fillers like `也就是说`

## Trust Signals To Preserve

- "I have seen this repeatedly"
- "Here is where people get fooled"
- "Here is what the data looks like on the surface"
- "Here is the actual downstream damage"

## Failure Modes

Avoid these degradations:

- making the copy too neat
- adding inspirational filler
- sounding like a sales funnel
- replacing sharp lines with generalized summaries
- overexplaining every term and killing the rhythm
